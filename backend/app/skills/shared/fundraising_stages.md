# Fundraising Stages — What Investors Expect

## Pre-Seed

### What You Need
- Compelling founder story and domain expertise
- Clear problem statement with evidence of pain
- Initial hypothesis for solution approach
- Small team (1-3 founders) with complementary skills
- Prototype or mockup (not necessarily working product)

### What Investors Ask
- "Why are you the right team to solve this?"
- "How do you know this is a real problem?"
- "What have you learned from talking to potential customers?"
- "What are your key assumptions and how will you test them?"

### Typical Metrics
- Customer discovery interviews completed (aim for 50+)
- Waitlist signups or LOIs
- No revenue expectation

### Red Flags at This Stage
- No customer conversations
- Solution looking for a problem
- Solo non-technical founder for a technical product
- Unrealistic timeline expectations

---

## Seed

### What You Need
- Working product (MVP at minimum, beta ideally)
- Early traction: first paying customers or strong engagement metrics
- Evidence of product-market fit signal (not proven PMF)
- Clear go-to-market hypothesis being tested
- 2-4 person team with key roles covered

### What Investors Ask
- "Show me your early traction — users, revenue, engagement"
- "What does your retention look like for early cohorts?"
- "How did you acquire your first customers?"
- "What's your path to $1M ARR?"
- "Why will this be a $100M+ company?"

### Typical Metrics
- $0–$500K ARR (or strong engagement if pre-revenue)
- 10–100 paying customers
- Month-over-month growth of 15-30%
- Early retention signal (>40% D30 for consumer, >80% month 3 for B2B)

### Red Flags at This Stage
- Product exists but no real users
- High churn in early cohorts
- All growth from paid acquisition with poor CAC/LTV
- No clear differentiation from existing solutions

---

## Series A

### What You Need
- **Proven product-market fit**: Customers love the product and stay
- **Repeatable revenue engine**: You know how to acquire customers profitably
- **Unit economics that work**: LTV/CAC > 3x, payback < 18 months
- **Clear path to $100M ARR**: Bottom-up model showing how you get there
- **Scalable team**: Key hires in engineering, sales/marketing, and ops

### What Investors Ask
- "Walk me through your unit economics"
- "What's your CAC by channel and how has it trended?"
- "Show me cohort retention curves"
- "What happens when you pour more money into growth?"
- "Who are your next 10 hires and why?"
- "What does the competitive landscape look like in 3 years?"

### Typical Metrics
- $1M–$5M ARR (median ~$2M at Series A)
- 100+ customers
- NRR > 100%
- Logo churn < 3% monthly
- 2-3x year-over-year growth
- LTV/CAC > 3x

### Red Flags at This Stage
- Revenue growth but terrible retention
- Over-reliance on a single large customer (>25% of revenue)
- Unit economics that don't improve with scale
- No clear competitive moat
- Founder unable to articulate the "why now"

---

## Series B

### What You Need
- **Scaling proof**: Revenue growth accelerating or maintaining at scale
- **Market leadership signal**: Becoming the category leader or strong #2
- **Operating leverage**: Margins improving, processes systematized
- **Expansion potential**: New products, segments, or geographies identified
- **Strong management team**: VP-level leaders in key functions

### What Investors Ask
- "How do you become a $1B company?"
- "What's your expansion strategy — new products, segments, or geos?"
- "Show me your operating leverage — how do margins trend?"
- "What are the biggest risks to your growth trajectory?"
- "How does your competitive moat deepen over time?"

### Typical Metrics
- $10M–$30M ARR
- NRR > 120%
- Burn multiple < 2x
- Rule of 40 approaching or meeting threshold
- Clear market leadership in core segment

---

## Growth / Late Stage

### What You Need
- Dominant market position or clear path to it
- Profitability or clear path to profitability
- Multiple growth vectors (products, markets, segments)
- World-class executive team
- IPO-readiness or strategic acquisition potential

### Typical Metrics
- $50M+ ARR
- Improving margins
- Sustainable growth rate (20-40%+ at scale)
- Market leadership in core vertical
